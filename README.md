# Customer-Churn-Analysis
Customer Churn Analysis using Python, Pandas, and Matplotlib to identify churn patterns and provide business recommendations.

Project Overview
Customer churn is one of the most important business challenges for subscription-based companies. Losing existing customers increases acquisition costs and reduces long-term profitability.
This project analyzes customer behavior using Python to identify the key factors associated with customer churn and provide actionable business recommendations.

Business Problem
The company is experiencing a high customer churn rate.
The objective of this project is to identify the factors contributing to churn and recommend strategies that improve customer retention.

Key Findings
1. High Payment Delay
Customers who churned delayed their payments for an average of 22.33 days, compared to 12.45 days for retained customers.

2. Frequent Support Calls
Churned customers contacted customer support significantly more often than retained customers.

3. Monthly Contracts
Monthly contracts recorded the highest churn rate (51.61%), while quarterly contracts had the lowest churn.

4. Customer Spending
Customers with higher spending showed stronger retention compared to lower-spending customers.

5. Subscription Type
Subscription type showed only a minor effect on customer churn.

High-Risk Customer Profile
A customer is more likely to churn if they:
Has a monthly contract
Frequently contacts customer support
Delays payments
Has relatively lower spending
Shows repeated service interactions

Business Recommendations
Encourage customers to switch to annual subscriptions through discounts.
Implement automated payment reminders.
Monitor customers with repeated support calls.
Improve customer support resolution times.
Create loyalty programs for long-term customers.
Develop a churn prediction dashboard for business teams.
